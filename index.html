The problem if you're technical

You can write code, train models, build pipelines. But biomedical research is a different world. The data is scattered across PubMed, ClinicalTrials.gov, and dozens of organism-specific databases. The papers are dense. The vocabulary is unfamiliar. You don't know what you don't know.

Where do I start?

PubMed returns 1,200 results. Which 20 matter? Which are reviews vs original research? Which are relevant to drug discovery vs basic biology?

What am I reading?

A paper mentions MSH3, MutSbeta, somatic CAG expansion. You understand the words individually. Together they mean nothing to you yet.

What can I actually do?

You want to contribute, not just read. But the gap between "I have a GPU" and "I can screen drug candidates" feels enormous.

What this tool does

Biotech Research Studio is the bridge. It takes your technical skills and applies them to published research. Gemma reads the papers. The platform structures the output. You get data you can work with.
